Prescriptive Analysis

Prescriptive analytics is the most sought after, but only a few organisations are able to perform it. It is the frontier of data analysis and utilises state of the art technology and data practices. It combines the insight developed from all the previous analyses to establish a course of action regarding problems or decision-making (Gibson, n.d.). This is achieved by examining the data and information regarding feasible situations or scenarios, available sources, past and current performance, and suggests a course of action or strategy. It may be utilised for the immediate and long term future (Segal, 2019).

Prescriptive analytics filters through the clutter of present uncertainties and changing conditions. This helps to prevent fraud, increase efficiency, meet performance targets, and limit risk. It is not foolproof and relies on an organisation’s ability to ask the right questions and react appropriately to the answers. Thus, the accuracy of the results and its effectiveness is dependent on the quality of input and validity of the assumptions provided (Segal, 2019).

Rather than relying on averages, organisations may utilise prescriptive analytics to simulate the probabilities of various outcomes, further improving their understanding regarding the risk levels and uncertainties being faced. Ultimately it provides the organisation with an improved understanding of the likelihood of worst-case scenarios occurring and equips them with the knowledge to plan accordingly (Segal, 2019).
