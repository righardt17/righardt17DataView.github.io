Diagnostic Analysis

Diagnostic analysis’ main objective is to derive an answer as to why certain events/problems occurred by identifying the causes thereof. This is achieved by using the insight gathered from statistical analysis (descriptive and inferential).

It is a valuable tool in terms of analysing the patterns that may exist in a dataset, which may then be documented and associated with the occurrence of an event/problem. These documented patterns will help to identify problems that may show resemblance and therefore be subject to a similar prescription (Guru99, 2019). Hence, the establishment of detailed information is critical to diagnostic analysis as this mitigates the iteration of work and interconnects problems – leading to time and cost savings. Examples of diagnostic analysis in business applications include:
  • A freight company investigating the cause of slow shipments in a specific region
  • A SaaS company establishing which marketing activities increased trials (Gibson, n.d.)
